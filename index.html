World Crime Statistics 👮

Crime knows no borders, and its impact reverberates around the world. In this visualisation, we distill complex crime statistics into clear, visual insights that uncover global crime patterns. Discover which regions are most affected, the world's crime rate across a period of time, and the statistics of prisoners in various regions across the world.

World Crime Index 2023

The crime index is an estimation of the overall level of crime in a given city or a country. The United States of America is reported to have the highest crime index in 2023, with a value of 2720.1.


World Crime Rate from 2000 to 2020

In between years 2000 to 2020, the global crime rate per 100K population peaked in the year 2001 (6.94%). In 18 years' time, the global crime rate has decreased by 1.33% (5.61% in 2020). This shows that the world's safety has improved over the years.


Prisoners in Various Regions (by Gender)

According to the split bar chart on the left, it can be seen that every year, the Americas has the most number of prisoners as compared to other regions. Moreover, there are generally much more male prisoners than female prisoners in each region.


Prisoners in Various Regions (by Age Group)

Approximately 98~99% of prisoners in each region are aged 18 and above, with the remaining 1~2% being teenagers.


Sentenced or Unsentenced?

In the years 2011 and before, more than 50% of the prisoners are sentenced in most of the regions. However, in the years 2012 and after, more than 50% of the prisoners are unsentenced in most of the regions. Despite so, Asia, Europe, and Oceania have higher percentage of sentenced prisoners than unsentenced prisoners as compared to other regions.
